2. Core Functions / Products / Business Workflow Understanding
Introduction to Procurement and Sourcing

Procurement and Sourcing are critical business functions responsible for acquiring goods and services required for organizational operations. While sourcing focuses on identifying and selecting the right suppliers, procurement encompasses the complete purchasing lifecycle, from requirement identification to payment processing and supplier performance evaluation. Together, these functions help organizations optimize costs, ensure quality, mitigate risks, and maintain uninterrupted business operations.

Key Objectives of Procurement and Sourcing

The primary objectives of procurement and sourcing are:

Acquiring products and services at the best possible value.
Ensuring timely availability of required materials and resources.
Building reliable supplier relationships.
Maintaining quality and compliance standards.
Reducing procurement costs and operational risks.
Improving supply chain efficiency and business continuity.
Core Functions of Procurement and Sourcing
1. Demand and Requirement Management

The procurement process begins when a business unit identifies a need for a product or service. Procurement teams work with stakeholders to understand specifications, quantities, budgets, and delivery timelines before initiating the sourcing process.

Key Activities:

Requirement gathering
Budget estimation
Demand forecasting
Approval workflows
2. Supplier Discovery and Evaluation

Organizations identify potential suppliers capable of meeting their business requirements. Suppliers are evaluated based on pricing, quality, delivery capabilities, certifications, and past performance.

Key Activities:

Supplier identification
Vendor qualification
Market research
Supplier risk assessment
3. Strategic Sourcing

Strategic sourcing involves selecting suppliers that provide the best balance between cost, quality, reliability, and long-term business value.

Key Activities:

Request for Quotation (RFQ)
Request for Proposal (RFP)
Supplier comparison
Contract negotiation
4. Purchase Order Management

Once a supplier is selected, procurement teams create and issue purchase orders specifying product details, quantities, pricing, delivery schedules, and contractual terms.

Key Activities:

Purchase requisition approval
Purchase order creation
Order tracking
Change management
5. Logistics and Shipment Management

After order placement, logistics operations ensure products are transported and delivered according to agreed schedules. Shipment visibility is essential to prevent delays and supply disruptions.

Key Activities:

Transportation coordination
Shipment tracking
Delivery monitoring
Freight management
6. Invoice Verification and Payment Processing

Supplier invoices are validated against purchase orders, contracts, shipment records, and agreed pricing terms before payments are approved.

Key Activities:

Invoice matching
Payment approval
Financial reconciliation
Dispute resolution
7. Supplier Performance Management

Organizations continuously monitor supplier performance to ensure compliance with quality standards, delivery commitments, and service level agreements (SLAs).

Key Activities:

Performance tracking
SLA monitoring
Quality assessment
Supplier scorecard generation
8. Contract and Compliance Management

Procurement teams ensure that all procurement activities adhere to contractual obligations, internal policies, and regulatory requirements.

Key Activities:

Contract lifecycle management
Compliance audits
Risk monitoring
Penalty and claim management
Products and Services Typically Procured

Organizations procure a wide range of products and services depending on their industry and operational needs.

Products

End-to-End Procurement Workflow

The procurement workflow follows a structured sequence:

Requirement Identification → Supplier Sourcing → Supplier Evaluation → Contract Negotiation → Purchase Order Creation → Shipment and Delivery → Invoice Verification → Payment Processing → Supplier Performance Review

This workflow ensures that organizations obtain the right products and services from the right suppliers at the right price and time while maintaining compliance and operational efficiency.

Business Value of Procurement and Sourcing

Effective procurement and sourcing practices provide several organizational benefits:

Cost optimization and spend control
Improved supplier relationships
Enhanced supply chain visibility
Reduced procurement risks




3. Roles in the Procurement and Sourcing Workflow
Introduction

Procurement and sourcing involve multiple stakeholders working together to ensure that goods and services are acquired efficiently, cost-effectively, and in compliance with organizational policies. Each role has specific responsibilities that contribute to the successful execution of the procurement lifecycle, from requirement identification to supplier performance evaluation.

1. Business Requestor / End User

The Business Requestor is the individual or department that identifies the need for a product or service. They initiate the procurement process by submitting purchase requests and providing detailed requirements.

Responsibilities
Identify business needs.
Raise purchase requisitions.
Define product or service specifications.
Provide budget justification.
Confirm successful delivery and acceptance.
2. Procurement Manager

The Procurement Manager oversees the overall procurement process and ensures that purchasing activities align with organizational goals, budgets, and policies.

Responsibilities
Approve procurement requests.
Manage procurement strategies.
Ensure cost optimization.
Monitor procurement performance.
Coordinate with stakeholders and suppliers.
3. Sourcing Specialist

The Sourcing Specialist focuses on identifying, evaluating, and selecting suppliers capable of meeting organizational requirements.

Responsibilities
Conduct supplier market research.
Identify potential vendors.
Manage RFQ/RFP processes.
Evaluate supplier proposals.
Support contract negotiations.
4. Supplier / Vendor

Suppliers are external organizations that provide goods or services to the company based on agreed contractual terms.

Responsibilities
Deliver products or services.
Adhere to contractual obligations.
Maintain quality standards.
Submit invoices.
Meet delivery timelines and SLAs.
5. Contract Manager

The Contract Manager is responsible for creating, monitoring, and maintaining procurement contracts throughout their lifecycle.

Responsibilities
Draft and manage contracts.
Track contract validity and renewals.
Monitor compliance with agreed terms.
Manage SLA commitments.
Handle disputes and amendments.
6. Logistics and Transportation Coordinator

This role manages the movement of goods from suppliers to the organization and ensures timely delivery.

Responsibilities
Coordinate transportation activities.
Monitor shipment status.
Track delivery schedules.
Manage freight costs.
Resolve transportation issues.
7. Finance and Accounts Payable Team

The Finance Team ensures that invoices are accurate and payments are processed according to contractual agreements.

Responsibilities
Verify invoices.
Perform PO-Invoice matching.
Process supplier payments.
Manage financial records.
Resolve billing discrepancies.
8. Quality Assurance Team

The Quality Assurance Team validates whether delivered goods or services meet predefined quality standards and specifications.

Responsibilities
Inspect received products.
Conduct quality checks.
Report defects or non-conformities.
Approve or reject deliveries.
Support supplier quality evaluations.
9. Compliance and Audit Officer

The Compliance Officer ensures that procurement activities comply with internal policies, industry regulations, and contractual obligations.

Responsibilities
Conduct procurement audits.
Verify regulatory compliance.
Monitor procurement risks.
Identify policy violations.
Support governance initiatives.
10. Executive Management

Executive leadership provides strategic direction and oversight for procurement activities, ensuring alignment with business objectives.

Responsibilities
Approve major procurement decisions.
Review procurement performance metrics.
Monitor cost savings initiatives.
Evaluate supplier risk exposure.
Support strategic sourcing initiatives.

5. Conclusion

Procurement and sourcing are essential business functions that ensure organizations acquire the right products and services at the right cost, quality, and time. These processes involve multiple stakeholders, including procurement teams, suppliers, finance departments, and logistics providers, working together to support efficient business operations.

With increasing supply chain complexity and growing business demands, organizations are adopting digital procurement solutions to improve supplier management, streamline purchasing activities, ensure contract compliance, and reduce operational risks. Effective procurement and sourcing practices not only optimize costs but also enhance transparency, supplier performance, and overall business efficiency, making them a critical component of organizational success.

Glossary of Terms
Term	Definition
Procurement	The process of acquiring goods and services required by an organization.
Sourcing	The process of identifying, evaluating, and selecting suppliers.
Supplier/Vendor	An external organization that provides goods or services.
RFQ (Request for Quotation)	A document used to request price quotations from suppliers.
RFP (Request for Proposal)	A document inviting suppliers to propose solutions and pricing.
Purchase Requisition (PR)	An internal request to purchase goods or services.
Purchase Order (PO)	An official document issued to a supplier specifying purchase details.
Contract Management	The process of creating, monitoring, and managing supplier contracts.
SLA (Service Level Agreement)	A formal agreement defining expected service performance levels.
Invoice	A bill submitted by a supplier requesting payment for delivered goods or services.
Supplier Evaluation	Assessment of suppliers based on quality, cost, delivery, and performance.
Spend Management	Monitoring and controlling organizational purchasing expenses.
Logistics	The process of transporting and delivering goods from suppliers to customers.
Freight Management	Managing transportation costs and shipment activities.
Compliance	Adherence to contractual, organizational, and regulatory requirements.
Supplier Performance Management	Monitoring and evaluating supplier performance against agreed standards.
Strategic Sourcing	Long-term supplier selection focused on cost optimization and business value.
Supply Chain	The network involved in producing, transporting, and delivering products or services.
Contract Compliance	Ensuring suppliers and buyers adhere to agreed contract terms and conditions.
Financial Leakage	Revenue loss caused by overbilling, duplicate payments, pricing errors, or contract violations.
